Past Projects

For my final project in my database course, me and my group decided to create a database for the Seattle Kraken franchise by manually inputting the data for each player on the team. We were then able to use this data to create schemas to pull out certain players depending on statistics, for instance, listing all players who have scored more than a certain number of goals that season. This could be useful to someone who would manage the team because they can easily see what each players strength is and how they can be used when determining which line to put them on.

In 2021, I decided to do some research on the different methods of monitoring network traffic as a final project for my computer systems course. I did some research on four different methods: using a firewall, SNMP monitoring, passive analysis and active monitoring. At the end, I attempted to rank them by their effectiveness by weighing each ones advantages and disadvantages.

ProLink was an idea for an app created in the summer of 2020. The app would connect amateur golfers to teaching professionals at local courses so that they could review a short video uploaded by the amateur of their swing. The course pro would then be able to make some quick comments on their swing as a way for amateur golfers to get quick and cheap golf advice without having to drive to the course.

Due to the surge of Web3 and blockchain technology, my colleagues and I decided to create a ratings system for non-fungible tokens on OpenSea as a project for my software engineering course. The final prototype is in the form of a browser extension, which generates a rating for both the seller as a whole and the seller's product using a set of fixed data which we manually input.

During the summer of 2022, I took three online development course through Robocorp. The purpose of this was to improve my work with Robotic Process Automation (RPA) Development. The first course centered around the basics of building a robot and allowed me to create a program that would take data from an excel file and enter it into an online form. The second course was similar, but involved us creating our own robot which would fill out a form online and then save and zip the receipts. The third course involved the basics around the producer-consumer model of robots, as well as an introduction into work items. The entire course used Selenium RPA Framework and a little bit of Python to complete the robots.
